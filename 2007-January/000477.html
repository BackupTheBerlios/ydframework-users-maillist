Hi all,

if I read through the emails correctly, it seems that most people
favor http://www.simplemachines.org. I suggest we just go for it and
see how far we get.

On the other hand, are there people interested in writing some reviews
about the framework? I think we are now at a point where some
marketing could definitely help us forward.

If I look at what would be helpful if someone could take care of it,
then I would say:
- Somebody that takes care of announcing new releases to e.g.
freshmeat.net and other PHP interested websites
- Somebody that can take care of vamping up the documentation and help
writing some of the missing parts
- Someone that can help in looking forward and helping to find out
what PHP5 could help us improve on the framework and what other things
we should put in the pipeline.
- Someone that can take care of following up the bug reports and
feature requests

I'm still convinced we have a framework that is one of the better ones
written in PHP, but without a full blown team supporting it, we will
keep on being a small fish in the big sea...

cheers,


pieter
